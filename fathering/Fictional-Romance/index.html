Synopsis

In a world where memories fade like morning mist and shadows whisper forgotten truths, two souls are bound by an ancient river's bargain.

Elion was born under a sky that bled fire, marked by a broken crown he cannot understand. He doesn't bleed red—he bleeds light, golden and shimmering, carrying the weight of time itself. Split across seven names he cannot remember, he wanders a kingdom slowly forgetting its own existence.

Saela, the girl they call Whisperwitch, has heard the voices of shadows since the day her twin sister died. She speaks to the darkness, and it answers—until the night she meets a boy whose very existence makes the shadows recoil in fear.

"Take one child," she said, "and give me one who will live."
The river listened. It always did.

As the Veil of forgetting spreads across the land, erasing names, faces, and entire histories, Elion and Saela must unravel the mystery of his fractured identity and her forbidden gift. Their growing love becomes both anchor and key—the only force strong enough to break a bargain made before time began.

Excerpt from Chapter One

The night Elion was born, the sky bled flame—gold and crimson streaks tearing through the clouds as if Heaven itself had been ripped open. The elders called it a comet storm. The watchers called it prophecy.

But to Mira, high priestess of the Ninth Moon and keeper of the River Oru, it was a debt being collected.

"Elion," she whispered into the dark, naming a child not yet born. "Elion of the fire, son of the forgotten vow... may you never learn who you truly are."

Because if he did—if he remembered what had been taken from him—the world might never survive it.

Main Characters

Elion

Born under prophecy, marked by mystery. His blood carries the light of forgotten stars and the weight of seven lives he cannot remember.

Saela

The Whisperwitch who speaks to shadows. Her gift is both blessing and curse, revealing truths that were meant to stay buried.

The River Oru

Ancient beyond memory, it makes bargains and keeps debts. What it gives, it always claims back—with interest.

Themes & Allegory

The River Between Stars weaves profound spiritual themes through its fantasy narrative:

  • Memory as Sacred: The power and responsibility of remembering in a world that forgets
  • Love as Anchor: How connection grounds us when everything else fades
  • The Price of Bargains: What we sacrifice for survival and whether any deal is truly worth it
  • Identity and Fragmentation: Finding wholeness when split across impossible choices
  • Faith in Darkness: Trusting what we cannot see, even when shadows speak louder than light
